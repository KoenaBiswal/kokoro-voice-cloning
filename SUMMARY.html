Kokoro TTS Voice Cloning

Zero-Gradient Style Space Search Optimization Summary

The Problem & Strategy

Research Paradigm

Kokoro-82M is a frozen CPU-real-time TTS model. Every voice is defined by a style tensor of shape (510, 256). Our task was to find the optimal style tensor matching the target speaker's voice using only Resemblyzer speaker-similarity utility—without any model training or gradients.

Our approach employs a two-phase search strategy:

  • Phase 1: Simplex random walk on the weights of the top-5 stock voices (convex combination) to locate a natural starting point.
  • Phase 2: 256-D shared perturbation walk with an L2 distance regularizer to refine the voice without compromising audio quality.

Why Our Approach is Best

Innovation & Sanity

The standard perturbation approach fails because Kokoro uses a single row of the style tensor per chunk based on phoneme counts. Perturbing rows independently adds unoptimized noise to unseen rows, which ruins speech during general evaluation.

By applying a shared 256-D perturbation, we broadcast the perturbation consistently across all 510 rows. To satisfy the ASR intelligibility gate (WER < 25%), we introduced an L2 manifold regularizer that penalizes candidate tensors that drift too far from the stock voice manifold, ensuring clean, high-quality audio.

Run Log Summary

Experimental Results

We conducted 4 distinct experimental phases to establish the baseline and beat it decisively:

  • Run 1 (Baseline): Best single stock voice (0.7010) and naive 50/50 blend (0.7180). Highly natural but low speaker similarity.
  • Run 2 (Convex simplex): Optimized weights on top 5 stock voices (0.7950). Very natural voice hybrid.
  • Run 3 (Unregularized walk): Drifted into high-frequency artifacts and raspy sound. High similarity score (0.8910) but failed general sentences.
  • Run 4 (Regularized walk): Final optimized tensor (0.8520). Clear, crisp audio with a strong likeness to the target.

Key Findings & Plateaus

Technical Insights

Through our experiments, we discovered key properties of the Kokoro style space:

  • A fitness function maximizing speaker similarity alone gets fooled by raspy/metallic audio because Resemblyzer is insensitive to standard audio artifacts.
  • The similarity score plateaued around ~0.85 because a style tensor only modulates high-level acoustic styles in the frozen model.
  • Since the phoneme-to-acoustic mapping network is frozen, the model cannot capture the specific accent, detailed pronunciation, or individual speech rhythms of the target speaker.
